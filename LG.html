Farming

Farming is the practice of cultivating soil, growing crops, and raising livestock for food, fiber, and other products. It involves various techniques and technologies to optimize production and sustainability. Farming can be categorized into subsistence (small-scale for local consumption) and commercial (large-scale for profit). Key factors include soil health, water management, and crop rotation. Modern farming increasingly incorporates innovations like precision agriculture, biotechnology, and sustainable practices to address challenges such as climate change and food security.

Cereals

Cereals are grasses cultivated for their edible seeds, known as grains. Major types include wheat, rice, corn (maize), barley, oats, and sorghum. They are staple foods for a large portion of the global population, providing essential carbohydrates, proteins, and nutrients. Cereals are used in various forms, from whole grains to processed products like flour, cereals, and snacks. They play a crucial role in agriculture, economies, and diets worldwide, and are also important for livestock feed. Sustainable farming practices are increasingly being adopted to enhance cereal production while minimizing environmental impact.
